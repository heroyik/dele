3. Essential Grammar Patterns
   The vocabulary inventory highlights several specific grammatical structures you must master.
   A. Verbs of Being and State (Ser vs. Estar vs. Tener)
   • Ser (Identity/Characteristics): Used with descriptions like alto, bajo, guapo, feo, joven, viejo14 and nationality español, mexicano4.
   • Estar (State/Mood): Used with feelings like triste, contento, enfadado, nervioso2 or physical states like cansado3.
   • Tener (Possession/Sensations): Used for age (tener... años)4, physical traits (tener barba, tener ojos azules)1, and sensations (tener calor, frío, sueño, sed, hambre)3.
   B. Likes and Dislikes
   • Gustar / Encantar: The source lists these explicitly under feelings2.
       ◦ Structure: Me gusta el chocolate / Me encantan los viajes.
   C. Common Prepositions & Collocations
   • En (Location/Transport):
       ◦ ir en coche, en metro, en autobús, en avión, en barco1517.
       ◦ vivir en una casa18.
   • A (Direction):
       ◦ ir a la playa, a la montaña16.
       ◦ ir al cine, al teatro19.
   • De (Origin/Material/Possession):
       ◦ ser de Laos, de Bali4.
       ◦ zumo de naranja10.
       ◦ tarta de chocolate10.
   D. Reflexive Verbs (Daily Routine) The source lists standard reflexive actions for daily routines920:
   • Levantarse (to get up)
   • Ducharse (to shower)
   • Lavarse (to wash oneself)
   • Peinarse (to comb one's hair)

4. Study Tips and Strategies
   Based on the organization of the Cervantes inventory, here is a suggested study strategy:
1. Thematic Blocking: The syllabus is strictly divided by topic (e.g., Housing, Health, Shopping). Study one "block" per week. For example, when studying Housing (Vivienda)18..., learn the furniture (mesa, silla), the rooms (cocina, baño), and the verbs (alquilar, compartir) together.
1. Verb-Noun Pairing: Do not learn verbs in isolation. Learn them with the nouns provided in the source:
       ◦ Don't just learn hacer (to do/make). Learn: hacer la cama (make the bed), hacer la compra (do the shopping)24, hacer deporte (do sports)9.
       ◦ Don't just learn tomar (to take/have). Learn: tomar un café19, tomar el autobús17.
1. Opposites (Antonyms): The inventory often lists adjectives in pairs. This is a high-yield memory technique.
       ◦ Limpio / Sucio (Clean/Dirty)22
       ◦ Caro / Barato (Expensive/Cheap)25
       ◦ Rico / Pobre (Rich/Poor)26
       ◦ Abierto / Reservado (Open/Reserved)2
